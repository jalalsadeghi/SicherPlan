# Codex Prompt — US-30-T2

## Task title

**Compare assigned, actual, and approved hours/costs against partner commercial settings.**

## Objective

Add deterministic commercial comparison logic to subcontractor invoice checks so expected and approved costs can be resolved from released assignments, approved actuals, and partner rate settings without moving commercial ownership out of the partner domain.

## Source context

- Updated proposal: subcontractor master/commercial terms include function-based rates and invoice options; subcontractor settlement compares planned work, attendance evidence, and actual hours before invoice approval.
- Implementation specification: partner finance profile and rate-card tables are defined in the subcontractor context, while finance invoice checks read those settings; `finance.subcontractor_invoice_check_line` persists expected/approved quantities and amounts plus variance reason.
- Earlier uploaded subcontractor-management materials emphasized company commercial terms, scope, function-based rates, shift controlling, and invoice verification visibility.
- Sprint reference: `docs/sprint/sprint-10-payroll-billing-and-settlement.md`.
- Cross-cutting rules: `AGENTS.md`, especially bounded-context ownership, finance-bridge discipline, and centralized temporal overlap handling.

## Dependencies

- `US-13-T1` should already provide subcontractor finance profile and rate-card structures or equivalent partner-owned commercial settings.
- `US-14-T4` should already provide partner-side read models that identify workforce/function/qualification context needed for cost resolution.
- `US-30-T1` should already provide invoice-check headers/lines and the basic aggregation workflow.
- `US-19-T1` through `US-19-T3` plus `US-26-T2`/`US-27-T2` should already provide assigned, actual, and approved quantities for comparison.

## Scope of work

- Implement comparison and rate-resolution services that can derive expected and approved subcontractor cost from partner rate cards, finance profile settings, assignment context, actual quantities, and approved outcomes.
- Resolve function, qualification, billing-unit, and effective-date dimensions against partner rate cards without duplicating ownership of partner commercial master data inside finance.
- Populate or refresh invoice-check-line fields for expected quantity/amount, approved quantity/amount, and structured variance reasons such as missing rate, quantity mismatch, canceled/replaced shift, or submitted-amount discrepancy according to repository conventions.
- Support comparison between assigned hours, actual hours, and approved hours/costs at both header and line level, preserving explainability for controller review.
- Handle incomplete commercial configuration explicitly through flags, errors, or review-required states rather than silently zeroing out expected cost.
- Add automated tests for rate resolution, effective-date selection, missing-rate scenarios, quantity comparison, variance-reason classification, and tenant-safe partner/finance joins.

## Preferred file targets

- `modules/finance/` subcontractor-cost resolution services, schemas, and tests.
- Read-adapter helpers that consume partner-owned finance profile/rate-card data without violating ownership.
- Optional shared temporal-validation or pricing-resolution helpers only if they do not blur CRM vs partner commercial ownership.
- Tests covering cost resolution, edge cases, and variance classification.

## Hard constraints

- Partner commercial settings remain partner-owned. Finance may read and resolve them, but must not duplicate or redefine them as new master truth.
- Do not compare cost from raw time events; use approved finance actuals and released assignment context.
- Missing or ambiguous rate resolution must be explicit and auditable, not silently flattened into zero or generic totals.
- Keep quantity and amount comparison logic deterministic and test-covered across effective dates and pricing dimensions.
- Do not move controller workflow, notes, or portal exposure into this task; keep the focus on comparison logic and data enrichment.
- Avoid hiding the comparison engine inside UI-only code or manual export scripts.

## Expected implementation outputs

- Partner-cost resolution and comparison services used by subcontractor invoice checks.
- Structured variance-reason enrichment on invoice-check lines and headers.
- Tests proving rate resolution, missing-configuration handling, and quantity/amount comparison behavior.
- Clear seams for later controller UI and portal visibility tasks.

## Non-goals

- Do not build the controller review UI here; that belongs to `US-30-T3`.
- Do not expose invoice-check comparison detail to the subcontractor portal here; that belongs to `US-30-T4`.
- Do not re-own partner rate cards or finance profile data inside finance tables beyond the issued/check result fields it truly owns.
- Do not implement accounting postings, payment runs, or GL integration in this task.

## Verification checklist

- [ ] Expected and approved costs resolve deterministically from partner rate settings and finance/assignment context.
- [ ] Missing or ambiguous rate scenarios produce explicit review signals rather than silent bad data.
- [ ] Line-level variance reasons are populated in a structured, test-covered way.
- [ ] Header totals reconcile with the persisted line-level comparison results.
- [ ] Tests cover effective-date selection, missing-rate handling, quantity mismatch, and tenant safety.
