# Codex Prompt — US-29-T2

## Task title

**Implement customer invoices, invoice lines, invoice numbering, layouts, and PDF output.**

## Objective

Generate reproducible customer invoices from released finance and CRM context so invoice numbers, line items, layout selection, totals, and archived PDFs all remain traceable to source actuals and released commercial rules.

## Source context

- Updated proposal: section 5.2 commercial/billing profile includes invoice addresses, layouts, invoice email, e-invoice switch, Leitweg ID, shipping and dunning control; section 5.6 requires customer billing by shift/object/event/patrol with separate lines and layouts; workflow 6.1 finishes with released timesheets and invoices.
- Implementation specification: section 4.8 defines `finance.customer_invoice` and `finance.customer_invoice_line`; section 5 keeps the customer commercial chain explicit and says finance reads customer commercial settings without owning them; generated PDFs should be represented as `docs.document` rows.
- Earlier uploaded customer-management materials emphasized commercial profile, invoice parties, layout options, and customer-facing billing outputs that the updated proposal absorbed.
- Sprint reference: `docs/sprint/sprint-10-payroll-billing-and-settlement.md`.
- Cross-cutting rules: `AGENTS.md`, especially docs linkage, finance-bridge discipline, and role-scoped customer visibility.

## Dependencies

- `US-29-T1` should already provide released timesheets and line-level linkage to approved actual records.
- `US-8-T1` through `US-8-T4` should already provide customer billing profile, invoice parties, rate/surcharge settings, layout codes, e-invoice toggles, and related commercial configuration.
- `US-6-T1` should already provide document storage and revision-safe file tracking for generated PDFs.
- If the repository already has a number-range or document-generation helper from earlier output tasks, reuse it instead of inventing an incompatible invoice-number or PDF pattern.

## Scope of work

- Implement or complete migrations, models, Pydantic schemas, services, and APIs for customer invoices and invoice lines in the finance bounded context.
- Support tenant-scoped deterministic invoice numbering, invoice status lifecycle, invoice date/due-date fields, currency, subtotal/tax/total fields, and linkage to the relevant customer, invoice party, and optional timesheet.
- Generate invoice lines from released timesheet lines or source actuals according to released CRM commercial rules, preserving optional `source_actual_id` linkage where the normalized schema expects it.
- Resolve layout codes and render or stage revision-safe invoice PDFs, linking the generated file to `docs.document` and back to the invoice row.
- Persist invoice amounts explicitly; do not leave critical commercial math hidden only in PDF templates or presentation layers.
- Add automated tests for invoice-number uniqueness, line ordering, amount/tax persistence, layout selection, PDF/document linkage, and source-actual traceability.

## Preferred file targets

- `modules/finance/` invoice models, schemas, services, routers, PDF-generation helpers, and tests.
- Alembic migrations for invoice/invoice-line tables or status/index changes where needed.
- Document-generation or PDF-template files that align with existing docs service conventions.
- Optional finance-admin endpoints or Vben integration points only if the repository already supports invoice maintenance surfaces.

## Hard constraints

- Finance reads customer commercial settings from CRM; do not duplicate ownership of billing profile, invoice parties, or commercial rules inside invoice master tables.
- Invoice numbers must be unique and deterministic according to tenant and repo numbering rules.
- Generated invoice PDFs must be linked as documents with durable business references.
- Do not bury invoice calculation results only in PDFs or front-end code; persist the financial totals and lines in the database.
- Do not trigger dispatch/e-invoice side effects inline in invoice-creation transactions; that belongs to later delivery/integration flows.
- Customer-facing invoice outputs must remain traceable to released timesheets/source actuals and approved commercial settings.

## Expected implementation outputs

- Customer-invoice and invoice-line persistence plus generation services.
- Tenant-scoped invoice numbering and lifecycle handling.
- Layout-aware PDF output linked through the docs service.
- Tests proving numbering, totals, source linkage, and PDF/document behavior.

## Non-goals

- Do not implement e-invoice delivery, dispatch messaging, or due-date enforcement workflows here; that belongs to `US-29-T3`.
- Do not expose invoices to the customer portal here; that belongs to `US-29-T4`.
- Do not introduce a separate invoice commercial-rules engine inside finance that conflicts with CRM-owned settings.
- Do not implement full accounts-receivable, dunning, or payment-reconciliation modules in this task.

## Verification checklist

- [ ] Invoices receive deterministic tenant-safe numbers and persist totals/line items in the database.
- [ ] Invoice lines remain traceable to source actuals or released timesheet context as appropriate.
- [ ] Layout selection and PDF generation/document linkage are reproducible and test-covered.
- [ ] Invoice creation does not inline provider dispatch side effects.
- [ ] Tests cover numbering, totals, line ordering, source linkage, and PDF/archive behavior.

## Codex response format

- A short implementation plan before touching files.
- A concise list of files created or modified.
- A summary of key design decisions and assumptions.
- Verification evidence: build/lint/test commands and notable outputs.
- Open follow-ups or blockers that should be carried into the next task.
