# Codex Prompt — US-29-T3

## Task title

**Support separate invoice addressees, dispatch rules, e-invoicing, and due-date control.**

## Objective

Complete invoice-delivery behavior so customer invoices honor separate invoice parties, payment terms, dispatch methods, e-invoicing settings, and due-date control through explicit finance/CRM contracts and adapter-safe integration flows.

## Source context

- Updated proposal: customer commercial/billing profile includes invoice addresses, invoice layouts, invoice email, e-invoice switch, Leitweg ID, shipping control, dunning control, tax and bank details, and payment terms; section 5.6 calls out separate invoice addressees and dispatch control.
- Implementation specification: `crm.customer_billing_profile` contains `e_invoice_enabled`, `leitweg_id`, `invoice_layout_code`, `shipping_method_code`, `dunning_policy_code`, and `payment_terms_days`; `crm.customer_invoice_party` holds alternative invoice addressees; finance invoice rows consume but do not own these settings.
- The technical architecture in the proposal routes payroll/billing integrations through REST/OpenAPI and integration adapters rather than ad hoc direct side effects.
- Sprint reference: `docs/sprint/sprint-10-payroll-billing-and-settlement.md`.
- Cross-cutting rules: `AGENTS.md`, especially docs linkage, outbox/integration patterns, and DE default / EN secondary behavior for any user-facing surfaces.

## Dependencies

- `US-8-T1` and `US-8-T4` should already provide invoice parties, billing profile fields, layout codes, e-invoice flags, and payment-term configuration.
- `US-29-T2` should already provide invoice persistence, numbering, totals, and PDF output.
- `US-6-T2` should already provide communication template/delivery patterns for non-provider dispatch states where messaging is needed.
- `US-6-T4` should already provide integration endpoints, jobs, and outbox workers for e-invoice or dispatch adapters.

## Scope of work

- Implement finance-side selection and validation for invoice addressees, dispatch/shipping method, e-invoice mode, Leitweg-style routing fields, due-date calculation from payment terms, and invoice-delivery status tracking.
- Resolve invoice party and billing profile data from CRM in a read-oriented way, then persist the chosen invoice-delivery context needed for the issued invoice without re-owning CRM master truth.
- Create adapter seams and integration handoff for e-invoicing or structured dispatch payloads, using outbox/jobs instead of direct network calls in the invoice-creation transaction.
- Support due-date control rules, delivery-state transitions, and validation for required-field combinations such as e-invoice enabled + required routing identifiers.
- Where the repository already has invoice-maintenance views, add narrow Vben-admin screens or panels for reviewing delivery context, due dates, and dispatch state with DE/EN localization and permission checks.
- Add tests for invoice-party resolution, due-date calculation, Leitweg/e-invoice validation, dispatch-state transitions, and integration handoff behavior.

## Preferred file targets

- `modules/finance/` invoice-delivery services, schemas, routers, and tests.
- Read-adapter or helper files that resolve CRM invoice-party and billing-profile data safely.
- Integration/outbox adapter wiring under the platform-services or infrastructure package.
- Optional Vben finance-admin delivery views/components if the repository already supports an invoice workspace.

## Hard constraints

- Do not move ownership of customer billing profiles or invoice parties out of CRM.
- Do not call e-invoice or dispatch providers directly from the same transaction that issues an invoice.
- Due-date calculation must be deterministic, test-covered, and grounded in persisted billing configuration rather than front-end-only math.
- Keep invoice-delivery state changes audit-safe and explicit; do not silently mutate issued invoices without status history or notes according to repo conventions.
- Do not expose internal delivery or finance details broadly to customer users in this task.
- Any added maintenance UI must follow German-default and English-secondary localization rules.

## Expected implementation outputs

- Invoice-party resolution and delivery-context handling in finance services.
- Due-date calculation and delivery-state tracking.
- Adapter-safe handoff for e-invoice or structured delivery workflows.
- Tests proving validation, handoff behavior, and deterministic due-date logic.

## Non-goals

- Do not build full dunning runs, receivables, or payment matching in this task.
- Do not expose invoices/timesheets in the customer portal here; that belongs to `US-29-T4`.
- Do not implement all provider-specific e-invoice standards in depth if the repository currently needs only stable adapter contracts and one representative path.
- Do not recalculate invoice totals here unless a narrow fix is needed to support delivery context.

## Verification checklist

- [ ] Invoice-party and billing-profile data resolve correctly and remain sourced from CRM ownership.
- [ ] Due dates are calculated deterministically from persisted settings and are test-covered.
- [ ] E-invoice/dispatch handoff uses integration/outbox patterns instead of inline provider calls.
- [ ] Validation catches incomplete delivery configurations such as missing routing IDs when e-invoice mode is enabled.
- [ ] Any added UI/API maintenance surface is permission-guarded and localized.

- Verification evidence: build/lint/test commands and notable outputs.
- Open follow-ups or blockers that should be carried into the next task.
